## Books

| Book | Author | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Digital Image Processing, 3rd Ed** | Gonzalez & Woods | Standard reference for image fundamentals. Images used in this repo (with citation). |
| **Computer Vision: Algorithms and Applications, 2nd Ed** (2022) | Richard Szeliski | Free PDF available online. Covers image formation through deep learning. Excellent comprehensive reference. |
| **Computer Vision: Models, Learning, and Inference** | Simon Prince | Free PDF available online. Strong on probabilistic and mathematical foundations. |
| **Multiple View Geometry in Computer Vision** | Hartley & Zisserman | The reference for 3D vision, epipolar geometry, camera models. Advanced. |
| **Computer Vision: A Modern Approach** | Forsyth & Ponce | Broad textbook covering classical and modern approaches. |

## Recommended Learning Path

Given where this tutorial series sits (sensor physics → probability → linear algebra → matching → features → CNNs):

1. **Shree Nayar's YouTube** — most aligned with the first-principles approach here. Covers optics, sensors, image formation the same way this series builds it.
2. **MIT 6.801 (Horn)** — classical machine vision from the physics side. Horn literally wrote the book on shape from shading.
3. **Szeliski's book** — free, comprehensive reference to keep alongside the tutorials.
4. **CS231n** — after completing this series, when you reach CNNs.
